[Untitled]

Aerial hoop / projection mapping piece

Description/Narrative

This piece is about augmenting yourself, i.e., augmenting your mortal, flawed form with nontraditional or technological means. The shape of the hoop naturally evokes a portal and invites users to physically manipulate it. Users are rewarded for sitting inside the hoop and spinning the hoop by projected visualization.

Requirements:

Aerial hoop (lyra) and rotor rigged at least 12' tall either to built-in suspension point or aerial tripod/scaffold with white wall. Two projectors (for wall and floor). Two IR cameras (for wall and floor).

UX

As the user approaches the scene, they can discern that an image (a movie of an ambient setting) is being projected inside the hoop, i.e. the "portal".
(Cues to interact with the hoop may include a lit path or arrows between the portal and the user once the user approaches closer, or particles streaming out of the portal towards the user.)

    Interactive triggers from portal 1:
  • If the user chooses to sit inside the hoop, their figure is projected with wings.
  • If the user chooses to spin, particles will stream away from the hoop as if being pushed by force.


    Interactive triggers from portal 2:
  • A 10' tall humanoid wireframe/stylization of them will appear projected in the background (the golem).
  • If the user chooses to spin, stylized captures of the user will appear in icon form at the top of the projected screen. These captures will persist as animations until after the spinning has stopped, and will be uploaded to a photomosiac.
Ambient effects:
As an ambient interaction, when the user is in the hoop, the longest line (after a minimum length) will be emphasized via a background grating.
